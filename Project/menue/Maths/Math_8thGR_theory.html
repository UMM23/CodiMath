I. Basic combinatorial concepts

Multiplication and addition of possibilities
  • Unions - groups or rows of elements of one or several finite sets that satisfy certain conditions.
  • The number of elements involved in a compound is called the class of that compound.
  • Addition Rule: If an object a can be chosen in m ways and another object b in n ways other than the ways to choose a, then either object a or b can be chosen in m ways + n ways.
  • Multiplication Rule: If an object a can be selected in m ways and another object b in n ways other than the ways to select a, then either object a or b can be selected in m X n the way.
Permutations, Variations and Combinations
  • Permutations without repetition of n elements are called such compounds, in each of which all the given elements enter and differ only in the order of the elements.
  • Variations without repetition of n elements of the kth class (k <= n) are called such compounds, each of which contains k different elements from the given n and differ from each other either in the elements or in the order of the elements.
  • Combinations without repetition of n elements of the kth class (k <= n) are called such compounds, each of which contains k different elements from the given n and differ from each other by at least one element.

II. Vectors

Vectors
  • A segment of which one end is taken as the first and the other as the second is called a directed segment or vector.
  • Vectors that have the same direction and the same length are called equal vectors.
Addition, subtraction and multiplication of vectors.
  • The sum of two unidirectional vectors is a vector unidirectional with them and of length equal to the sum of the lengths of the two vectors.
  • The sum of two oppositely directed vectors is a vector in the same direction as the vector of greater length and with a length equal to the difference of the lengths of the two vectors.
  • Subtraction: a - b of the vectors a and b is called the vector a + (-b), that is, a - b = a + (-b).
  • The product k u of the vector u with the number k is called a vector with a length equal to the product of the length of u and the absolute value of k, in the same direction as u for k > 0 and opposite to u for k < 0.

III. Triangles and Trapeziums

Triangles
  • A segment that joins the midpoints of two of the sides of a triangle is called a midsegment in a triangle.
  • The point of intersection of the medians of a triangle is called the midcenter of the triangle.
Trapeziums
  • A quadrilateral in which only one pair of opposite sides are parallel is called a trapezoid.
  • A trapezoid with equal hips is called an isosceles trapezoid.
  • The segment that joins the midpoints of the hips of a trapezoid is called the median segment of the trapezoid.
