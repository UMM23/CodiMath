I. Variables and Constants

Variables

In mathematics, variables are symbols used to represent unspecified numbers or values. They can take on different values and are often denoted by letters such as x, y, or z.

Constants

Parameters

Parameters are variables that take on specific values depending on the context of a problem. They are denoted by letters and can represent constant values under certain conditions.

II. Rational Expressions

Definition

A rational expression is an algebraic expression made up of numbers, variables, and arithmetic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division). It may include variables in the denominator of fractions.

Whole Rational Expression

A whole rational expression is a rational expression where there is no division by a variable.

Fractional Rational Expression

A rational expression is a fraction in which both the numerator and the denominator are polynomials. For example, (3x + 2)/(x - 1) is a rational expression.

Numeric Value of an Expression

The numeric value of an expression is obtained by replacing the variables with given rational numbers and evaluating the expression.

Allowable Values

Allowable values refer to the values that the lettered quantities in a rational expression can take. They may be restricted by conditions such as avoiding division by zero.

III. Monomials

Definition

A monomial is a single term algebraic expression. For example, 5x, -3y, and 7 are all monomials.

Normal Form of a Monomial

The normal form of a monomial is the standard way of writing it with the coefficient first followed by the variables in alphabetical order. For example, 3x^2y is in normal form.

Coefficient of a Monomial

The coefficient of a monomial is the numerical factor. For example, in the monomial 7x^2, 7 is the coefficient.

Power of a Monomial

The power of a monomial is the sum of the exponents of all the variables. For example, in 4x^3y^2, the power is 3 + 2 = 5.

Similar Monomials

Similar monomials have the same variables raised to the same powers. For example, 5x^2 and -3x^2 are similar monomials.

IV. Polynomials

Definition

A polynomial is an algebraic expression made up of two or more monomials. For example, 3x^2 + 2x - 5 is a polynomial.

Normal Form of a Polynomial

The normal form of a polynomial is the standard way of writing it with the terms in descending order of their degrees. For example, x^3 + 2x^2 - x + 5 is in normal form.

Coefficients of a Polynomial

The coefficients of a polynomial are the numerical factors of each term. For example, in the polynomial 4x^2 + 3x - 7, the coefficients are 4, 3, and -7.

Degree of a Polynomial

The degree of a polynomial is the highest power of the variable in the polynomial. For example, the degree of 5x^3 + 4x^2 - 2 is 3.
