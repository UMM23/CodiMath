Theory

Rectangular Coordinate System

Definition
  • Two perpendicular number axes that have a common origin and the same unit segment.
Coordinates of a Point
  • Definition - The location of an arbitrary point A (xA; yA) relative to the drawn coordinate system is determined by the numbers xA and yA. These numbers are called the coordinates of the point.
    • abscissa – xA is called the abscissa of point A.
    • ordinate – yA is called the ordinate of point A.

Geometric Solids

Prisms
  • Definition - A prism is a solid object with two identical ends and flat sides. The sides are parallelograms, and the cross section along the length is the same all the way through.
  • Lateral Surface Area - For a right prism, the lateral surface area is the sum of the areas of the lateral faces, which are rectangles. The formula is \( S = P \cdot h \), where \( P \) is the perimeter of the base and \( h \) is the height.
  • Total Surface Area - The total surface area of a right prism is the sum of the lateral surface area and the areas of the two bases. The formula is \( S_{total} = S + 2B \), where \( B \) is the area of one base.
  • Volume - The volume of a right prism is the product of the base area and the height. The formula is \( V = B \cdot h \). For a rectangular prism (parallelepiped), \( V = a \cdot b \cdot c \), where \( a \), \( b \), and \( c \) are the dimensions of the rectangle. For a cube, \( V = a^3 \), where \( a \) is the side length.
  • Examples:
    • For a triangular prism with a base area of 10 cm² and height of 5 cm, the volume is \( V = 10 \cdot 5 = 50 \) cm³.
    • For a rectangular prism with dimensions 3 cm, 4 cm, and 5 cm, the volume is \( V = 3 \cdot 4 \cdot 5 = 60 \) cm³.
Pyramids
  • Definition - A pyramid is a solid with a polygonal base and triangular faces that converge at a point (the apex). A regular pyramid has a base that is a regular polygon and faces that are congruent triangles.
  • Lateral Surface Area - The lateral surface area of a regular pyramid is calculated using the formula \( S = \frac{1}{2} \cdot P \cdot k \), where \( P \) is the perimeter of the base and \( k \) is the slant height.
  • Total Surface Area - The total surface area of a regular pyramid includes the lateral surface area and the base area. The formula is \( S_{total} = S + B \), where \( B \) is the area of the base.
  • Volume - The volume of a regular pyramid is given by \( V = \frac{1}{3} \cdot B \cdot h \), where \( B \) is the base area and \( h \) is the height (the perpendicular distance from the base to the apex).
  • Examples:
    • For a square pyramid with a base side length of 4 cm and height of 6 cm, the base area is \( B = 4^2 = 16 \) cm², and the volume is \( V = \frac{1}{3} \cdot 16 \cdot 6 = 32 \) cm³.
Cylinders
  • Definition - A cylinder is a solid object with two parallel circular bases connected by a curved surface at a fixed distance. A right circular cylinder has bases aligned one directly above the other.
  • Lateral Surface Area - The lateral surface area of a cylinder is the area of the curved surface. The formula is \( S = 2\pi rh \), where \( r \) is the radius of the base and \( h \) is the height.
  • Total Surface Area - The total surface area of a cylinder includes the lateral surface area and the areas of the two bases. The formula is \( S_{total} = 2\pi rh + 2\pi r^2 \).
  • Volume - The volume of a cylinder is the product of the base area and the height. The formula is \( V = \pi r^2 h \).
  • Examples:
    • For a cylinder with a base radius of 3 cm and height of 7 cm, the volume is \( V = \pi \cdot3^2 \cdot 7 = 63\pi \) cm³.

Additional Topics

Fractions
  • Addition and Subtraction - Operations involving fractions, such as \( \frac{3}{4} + \frac{1}{2} \).
  • Multiplication and Division - Multiplying and dividing fractions, e.g., \( \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{6} \).
Decimals
  • Decimal Operations - Performing operations with decimals, e.g., \( 2.5 \times 1.2 \).
  • Decimal to Fraction Conversion - Converting decimals to fractions, e.g., \( 0.75 = \frac{3}{4} \).

Exercises

Exercise 1: Fractions

Simplify the following fraction: \( \frac{12}{36} \).

Answer: \( \frac{1}{3} \).

Exercise 2: Decimals

Perform the following operation: \( 3.5 + 1.25 \).

Answer: \( 4.75 \).

Exercise 3: Cylinder Calculation

Calculate the lateral surface area of a cylinder with radius \( r = 4 \) cm and height \( h = 10 \) cm.

Answer: \( S = 2\pi \cdot 4 \cdot 10 = 80\pi \) cm².

Exercise 4: Volume of a Prism

Find the volume of a right prism with a triangular base, where the base area \( B = 36 \) cm² and height \( h = 8 \) cm.

Answer: \( V = B \cdot h = 36 \cdot 8 = 288 \) cm³.
